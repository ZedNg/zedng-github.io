Polynomial Divination (2022)

“For longer than recorded history, humans have been devoted to an unquenchable desire to divine truth about the world around us: systems and forces that operate just beyond the reach of our control. This scenario is the wellspring of our cultural development, often coalescing across multiple domains of art, science, technology, and religion. The digital world provides boundless tools to simulate systems and forces, natural and imagined, with a high degree of fidelity. As with ancient tribal fires, we gather around the flickering pixels and voxels, drawing forth and projecting equal parts fascination and imagination.

‘Polynomial Divination’ is a meditation on this long-held dimension of human creativity and exploration – relying on a blend of computer-based rendering and a custom projection/lighting system.”

Project Description for Ars Electronica Garden Aotearoa 2022

The project was part of the Ars Electronica Garden Aotearoa 2022 exhibition with Zach Challies and Douglas Easterly.

We designed a virtual fire that follows and attracts individuals nearby. We positioned this installation in the centre of other artworks, creating a gathering spot for people to congregate. This concept pays homage to the time-honoured tradition of gathering around a hearth to share stories but with a futuristic twist.

We utilized TouchDesigner to create the flame and incorporated ultrasonic sensors along the sides of the container. My role involved embedding and integrating the sensors and their data into the simulation, allowing it to respond and be "attracted" to people.
