Frozen Moment (2019)
Click on image to view full size.

The initial idea started from seeing an animated image of a mobile phone dropping on the ground. The image makes me realise that a dropped technological device is pretty much a nightmare in the modern world in this day and age. We as people are so connected to our devices and the ability to have a “power of knowledge and information” is contained in a form that fits our hands. If the device breaks, the “power” is lost and destroyed.

I represented this using a translucent cube form that is dropped on the ground, breaking into pieces. The light represents the power of the cube. As the cube breaks, the power is diminished and lost. A blue colour palette is used as it is a peaceful and calm colour that provided a feeling of stillness The character was modelled with 3D-Coat and rendered in Autodesk Maya and Arnold Renderer.

The project was part of the course work of MDDN241: 3D Modelling and Animation II for the Bachelor of Design Innovation (BDI) at Victoria University of Wellington (VUW).
